# WEB-001 — Shockwave Coronary IVL Important Safety Information (ISI)

**Source:** https://shockwavemedical.com/isi/coronary-us/
**Accessed:** 2026-04-07
**Type:** Official Manufacturer Safety Information

---

## Indications
- Lithotripsy-enabled, low-pressure balloon dilatation of severely calcified, stenotic de novo coronary arteries prior to stenting

## Contraindications
- Not intended for stent delivery
- Not intended for use in carotid or cerebrovascular arteries

## Key Warnings
- Dissection/perforation risk increases in severely calcified lesions
- Mechanical pulses may cause atrial or ventricular capture in bradycardic patients
- Interaction with sensing capabilities possible in pacemaker/defibrillator patients
- Electrocardiographic rhythm and arterial pressure monitoring required

## Precautions
- Physician must be trained in angiography and intravascular coronary procedures
- Use only recommended balloon inflation medium
- Protect hydrophilic coating with normal saline or water only
- Anticoagulant therapy required
- Caution when treating previous stents within 5mm of target lesion
- May interfere with certain implanted electrical devices

## Re-insertion Rules
- **Shockwave C2/C2+: Cannot be re-inserted once withdrawn**
- **Shockwave C2 Aero: May be re-inserted up to 3 times per patient per instructions**

## Major Adverse Events Listed
- Vessel occlusion, perforation, rupture, dissection
- Myocardial infarction, stroke
- Emergency bypass surgery, cardiac tamponade
- Arrhythmia, hemorrhage, thrombus, and death

## Device-Specific Risks
- Balloon pressure loss linked to dissection
- Calcium length identified as dissection predictor
- Device malfunction or failure requiring surgical intervention possible
